Forest Fire Prediction - Feature Information

Temperature (°C): Higher temperatures increase the likelihood of fire ignition.
Valid range: 2.2 – 33.3
Low: 2–15 Medium: 15–25 High: 25–33

Humidity (%): Lower humidity increases fire risk, as vegetation dries faster.
Valid range: 15 – 100
Low: 60–100 Medium: 30–60 High: 15–30

Rainfall (mm): Rainfall reduces fire probability by moistening the ground and vegetation.
Valid range: 0.0 – 6.4
Low: 3–6.4 Medium: 1–3 High: 0–1

Wind Speed (km/h): Stronger winds spread fires more rapidly.
Valid range: 0.4 – 9.4
Low: 0.4–3 Medium: 3–6 High: 6–9.4

FFMC (Fine Fuel Moisture Code): Indicates dryness of fine fuels like grass and leaves.
Valid range: 15.9 – 96.2
Low: 15–60 Medium: 60–80 High: 80–96

DMC (Duff Moisture Code): Measures moisture in loosely compacted organic soil layers.
Valid range: 0.87 – 291.3
Low: 0–100 Medium: 100–200 High: 200–291

DC (Drought Code): Reflects deep soil and large fuel dryness (long-term drought).
Valid range: 5.98 – 860.6
Low: 0–300 Medium: 300–600 High: 600–860

ISI (Initial Spread Index): Represents expected fire spread rate at ignition.
Valid range: 0.0 – 56.1
Low: 0–10 Medium: 10–25 High: 25–56
